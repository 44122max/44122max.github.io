NEW BLOG!

4/21 Cheers log! Today I took the truck on a reasonable trip. I will no longer be providing fuel level updates from here on out because my fuel gauge is officially broken. I filled my tank since last time we have spoken. I am afraid of the future of this truck, the transmission line fitting has sprung a leak and I m not sure how much fluid I m losing but it must be a lot. Either way lets not dwell on that fact I took the truck for a sizable drive. I noticed it performs much better if you leave the truck in 3rd gear. I believe 3rd gear is 1:1 in terms of input to output shaft gear ratio. 3rd gear provides me with smooth acceleration with minimal slip. I mainly stayed on Shaker Blvd in my travels tonight, no highway driving to report this time around. My phone died mid drive so I had no music to choose from so that was a bummer. The radio was okay though, tuning in to Z107.9 was my radio station of choice as always. My cruise around was nice and smooth. I can t say that enough, my V8 rocks on the side streets accelerating 0-40 miles per hour like a champ. There was minimal traffic, maybe a few other cars on the road. Since the last blog I have installed an ignition switch in my truck so to turn the truck on you must first start by flipping a switch on the dashboard, and then when the truck is in neutral you hit a button I have installed on it to start the engine. It s very slick. I have future plans to upgrade this system and it s security. You will hear more about this in a future blog. Either way, back to the cruise. So last night I put a half a quart of transmission fluid in it and it shifts smoother now. I put about 15 or 20 miles on my truck I would like to say, nothing too serious. I was behind the wheel I d like to say for 45 minutes, or more. So, it might have been longer than that. The transmission ran smooth the entire time, however. I have lots of plans on future improvements I would like to make to my current setup. I also have installed door panels since the last entry. I have an issue to report on the dashboard. My voltage gauge, fuel gauge, and temperature gauge have all stopped working right. Here is a picture to show you:

 

 

These photos show my gauges which now read severely off, due to an apparent electrical issue which has gone undiagnosed. This also shows my button and switch operated ignition system for this truck. I like this feature as it is simple, and it functions reliably well under all circumstances. It surely beats having the dumb switch to flip under the dash. I also feature a picture of my trucks interior as it is reassembled. My dashboard as well as the knee bolster has been installed which gives my truck a whole new clean look which covers all the loose wires. I also installed the steering column trim panels which cover the ignition cylinder. I m glad my truck actually resembles a truck now. That s all I have for now, next time I ll share pictures of my door panels, get some high quality interior photos of my truck. And this will continue! Until next time, Cheers Log!

 

 

4/10 Hello Log. Today I drove for more than an hour uninterrupted. This was a marvelous experience.

 

 

4/11 Hello Log! Today I had a blast driving my truck downtown. Right now fuel is just under three quarters of a tank and the truck runs great, like a champion. I have no issues to report and I can declare today to be a misfire free day. I m thinking that the misfire events may have been related to air in the fuel lines. I have no clue when they were last filled completely like I have just recently, but I think that the fuel tank and fuel system have purged all air from the lines and I have myself now a perfectly nimble pickup truck to drive around. I did not encounter any issues with hesitation climbing harsh gradients. I drove from the east side to the west side back to the east side again. I made a short venture on the highway that did not exceed 60 miles per hour. I was simply at the mercy of the traffic in front of me but I kept it in 3rd gear and rolled it up the on ramp and simply maintained a speed of ~55 miles an hour for about 3 minutes of highway travel. I could have gone faster had conditions relented.

 

 

4/14 Cheers Log! That is my new daily lingo, which is how I m going to start out my Motorist Logs from here on out. I just got back from a 30-40 minute long drive with only one individual incident which did not result in total loss of engine momentum, but did result in engine sputtering. The root cause is still unknown. A good hypothesis of mine is the ignition coils. I could simply look over to Summit for the ignition system and see what they have to offer. Just an idea. I really wish I could fix this daggum misfire. Fuel right now is hovering around a half a tank. I have to report I've clocked nearly 100 miles of driving since having been home so it s safe to say that my truck has been starting to demonstrate some little bit of reliability which is what I so desperately crave. I may not keep this truck for the longest period of time but for the foreseeable future I do plan on keeping this truck and the upgrades that are going to be put into it are immense. I have a TH400 transmission that I will later install once I ve squeezed all the juice out of my 4l60E. I shall then replace it with my TH400. That transmission will handle quite a bit more power. All in due time. Patience is key.

 

 

4/15 Today I took my truck for its maiden voyage on the highway for real. I stopped in mayfield but I drove my truck on 271 for a little over 3 miles to get there. And a little over 3 miles back. The truck rides really nicely at about 70 miles per hour in overdrive. I like to put it into 3rd gear while I m merging so that way I can gather momentum all the way down the on ramp. The truck doesn t like accelerating in 4th gear, which is why I leave it in 3 until it is ready to be put into overdrive at about 70 miles per hour. I will probably repeat my experiment on a short highway journey over to Rachel s house. I topped off the transmission fluid before I left. It took half of a quart. The fuel is just under half of  a tank. I found the ride to be quite enjoyable. I stayed in the right lane the entire time. The 70 MPH on my dashboard probably translates to more like 65 miles per hour on the highway. My speedometer is rather off.
